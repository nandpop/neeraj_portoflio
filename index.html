01 / About

A brief introduction.

I am an Assistant Professor at the School of Data Science and Artificial Intelligence at IIT Guwahati, where I lead the Signal Processing and Intelligence (SPIN) Lab.

My research lives at the boundary between signal processing and human perception. I am interested in how the ear and brain extract meaning from sound, and how machines can be taught to do the same — sometimes in surprisingly different ways. My recent work spans talker change detection, spatial audio cognition, time-varying signal modelling, and respiratory sound analysis for healthcare.

Before joining IIT Guwahati, I was a scientist at the Fraunhofer Institute for Integrated Circuits in Erlangen, a CV Raman Postdoctoral Researcher at IISc Bengaluru, and a BrainHub Postdoctoral Fellow at Carnegie Mellon University.

02 / Research

Lines of inquiry.

i.

Talker Change Detection

Comparing how humans and machines notice a switch between speakers — and what the gap reveals about both.

ii.

Spatial Audio Cognition

Understanding how the auditory system localises and tracks moving sources, and modelling it computationally.

iii.

Respiratory Sound Analysis

Coswara and beyond: using cough, breath, and voice as a non-invasive window into respiratory health.

iv.

Time-Varying Signals

AM–FM decompositions, sinusoidal demodulation, and non-stationary models of speech and music.

v.

Event-Triggered Sampling

Sampling driven by signal events rather than the clock — efficient, information-rich, and biologically inspired.

vi.

Computational Acoustics

From room impulse responses to perceptual models of timbre and space in everyday listening.
