01 / About

A brief introduction.

I am an Assistant Professor at the Mehta Family School of Data Science & Artificial Intelligence at IIT Guwahati, with associate appointments at the Centre for Linguistic Science and Technology and the Manekshaw Centre of Excellence for National Security Studies and Research.

My research lives at the boundary between signal processing and human perception. I am interested in how the ear and brain extract meaning from sound, and how machines can be taught to do the same — sometimes in surprisingly different ways. My work spans audio signal processing for healthcare, human–machine audio perception, speech analysis and synthesis, and the mathematical foundations of sampling and reconstruction.

Before IIT Guwahati, I was a Research Scientist at the International Audio Laboratories, Fraunhofer IIS (Erlangen), a CV Raman Postdoctoral Fellow at IISc Bengaluru, and a BrainHub Postdoctoral Fellow at Carnegie Mellon University. I received my PhD and MTech (Research) from the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru, advised by Prof. T. V. Sreenivas.

02 / Research

Lines of inquiry.

i.

Audio for Healthcare

Coswara and DiCOVA — respiratory sound analysis for remote screening, point-of-care diagnostics, and breathing anomaly detection.

ii.

Human–Machine Perception

Talker change detection, sound lateralization, attention decoding — comparing how humans and models listen.

iii.

Speech Analysis & Synthesis

Time-varying modeling, AM–FM decompositions, low-resource Indian-language speech, and stuttered-speech understanding.

iv.

Sampling & Reconstruction

Event-triggered and signal-dependent sampling, time-frequency analysis, and quadratic-phase Fourier methods.

v.

Computational Linguistics

Acoustics and nasalance in North-Eastern languages, vowel harmony modeling, and language-technology resources.

vi.

Imaging

Despeckling of SAR images and photoacoustic image reconstruction with transformer-based architectures.
